Planning & Design

The website was planned using a mobile-first approach. Wireframes were created for each page ensuring consistent navigation and layout. The design is inspired by leading adoption websites (Lort Smith, AAPS, Dogs Home) to create a trustworthy, warm, and professional appearance.

Development & Testing

  • ✅ Images tested – all display with proper alt text.

Privacy Issue

Issue: If not securely protected, information entered into the contact form, including the user's name, email address, and comments, can be misused or become public.

Solution: A privacy notice is prominently provided on the Contact Us page, informing visitors that contact details will only be shared with the business to respond to enquiries and will not be passed on to third parties. Furthermore, there is no permanent storage of any personal data on the website, without a backend database, which means that the data is used only temporarily and safely for communications.


Security Issue

Issue:If this contact form is not properly validated, users might be able to leave it blank or fill it with malicious information, resulting in low quality data or possible security threats.

Solution: All required fields must be completed before submitting to the server – Solution: Client side JavaScript validation is used. Basic input handling helps to minimise the chances of entering bad data by limiting data that can be entered. Improvements would be beneficial in the future by incorporating CAPTCHA into the system to block bots and incorporating server-side validation to add more security and verify all user input.


Copyright Issue

Issue: It is possible that the use of copyrighted images without proper authorization could result in copyright violations and potential legal problems with the website.

Solution: To avoid this, all images used in the website are either royalty-free from Unsplash, Pexels or original designs. The logo and event posters are created using Canva. All images on this page have also had Alt text added to provide access for those who cannot view them and the sources of the images are identified on the page.


Censorship & Content Appropriateness

Issue: All content on the website needs to be suitable for a wide range of visitors, such as families, children and the general public. Unsuitable content (offensive language, sensitive topics, graphic images etc.) can have a negative impact on users and affect trust in the organization.

Solution: All content on the site is carefully checked to ensure it is safe and appropriate for all ages and family friendly to ensure this. All text is simple, respectful and all images are positive and non-graphical. No offensive, violent or sensitive information is allowed within the site. Rather it emphasizesAnimal Welfare, adoption awareness and responsible pet ownership. This will help foster a platform of trust for the community that can lead to community involvement and support the organisation's mission of ensuring that animals can find loving homes.
